{{ "Cooke, Roger" | person_tag: "Roger Cook" }}, who had byn with me from his 14 yeres of age till 28, of a melancholik nature, pycking and devising occasions of just cause to depart on the suddayn, abowt 4 of the clok in the afternone requested of me lycense to depart, wheruppon rose whott words between us; and he, imagining with hisself that he had the 12 of July deserved my great displeasure and finding himself barred from vew of my philosophicall dealing with Mr. Henrik, thowght that he was utterly recest from intended goodnes toward him. Notwithstanding {{ "Cooke, Roger" | person_tag: "Roger Cook" }} his unseamely dealing, I promised him, yf he used himself toward me now in his absens, {{ "GBP 100 pounds" | currency_tag: "one hundred pounds" }} as sone as of my own clene hability I myght spare so much; and moreover, if he used himself well in lif toward God and the world, I promised him some pretty alchimicall experiments, whereuppon he might honestly live.
